# Financial Metrics Reference

This reference guide defines standard financial metrics used in earnings report analysis.

## Income Statement Metrics

### Revenue Metrics
- **Total Revenue / Net Sales**: Total income from normal business operations
- **Revenue Growth (YoY)**: (Current Year Revenue - Prior Year Revenue) / Prior Year Revenue × 100%
- **Revenue Growth (QoQ)**: (Current Quarter Revenue - Prior Quarter Revenue) / Prior Quarter Revenue × 100%

### Profitability Metrics
- **Gross Profit**: Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold
- **Gross Margin**: Gross Profit / Revenue × 100%
- **Operating Income / EBIT**: Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
- **Operating Margin**: Operating Income / Revenue × 100%
- **Net Income**: Operating Income - Interest - Taxes +/- Non-operating items
- **Net Margin**: Net Income / Revenue × 100%

### Per-Share Metrics
- **EPS (Earnings Per Share)**: Net Income / Weighted Average Shares Outstanding
- **Diluted EPS**: Net Income / (Weighted Average Shares + Potential Dilutive Securities)

## Balance Sheet Metrics

### Assets
- **Total Assets**: Current Assets + Non-Current Assets
- **Current Assets**: Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, etc. (assets convertible to cash within 1 year)
- **Non-Current Assets**: Property, Plant, Equipment, Intangible Assets, etc.

### Liabilities
- **Total Liabilities**: Current Liabilities + Non-Current Liabilities
- **Current Liabilities**: Accounts Payable, Short-term Debt, etc. (due within 1 year)
- **Total Debt**: Short-term Debt + Long-term Debt

### Equity
- **Total Equity**: Total Assets - Total Liabilities
- **Book Value Per Share**: Total Equity / Shares Outstanding

## Cash Flow Metrics

### Operating Cash Flow (OCF)
- Cash generated from core business operations
- Key indicator of business sustainability

### Free Cash Flow (FCF)
- **FCF = Operating Cash Flow - Capital Expenditures**
- Critical for evaluating financial flexibility

### Capital Expenditures (CapEx)
- Funds spent to acquire, upgrade, or maintain physical assets

## Key Ratios

### Liquidity Ratios
- **Current Ratio**: Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- **Quick Ratio**: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities

### Leverage Ratios
- **Debt-to-Equity**: Total Debt / Total Equity
- **Interest Coverage**: Operating Income / Interest Expense

### Return Metrics
- **ROE (Return on Equity)**: Net Income / Total Equity
- **ROA (Return on Assets)**: Net Income / Total Assets
- **ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)**: NOPAT / Invested Capital

## Valuation Metrics

### Market-Based
- **P/E Ratio**: Stock Price / EPS
- **EV/EBITDA**: Enterprise Value / EBITDA
- **P/B Ratio**: Stock Price / Book Value Per Share

### Enterprise Value Components
- **Enterprise Value (EV)**: Market Cap + Total Debt - Cash & Equivalents
- **Market Cap**: Stock Price × Shares Outstanding

## Growth Metrics

### Compound Growth Rates
- **CAGR**: (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/n) - 1
  - Where n = number of periods

### Sequential vs. Annual
- **YoY (Year-over-Year)**: Compares same quarter across different years
- **QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter)**: Compares consecutive quarters
- **Seasonal adjustments** often needed for QoQ comparisons

## Segment Reporting

### Business Segment Metrics
- **Segment Revenue**: Revenue attributable to specific business line
- **Segment Operating Income**: Profitability by business segment
- **Segment Margin**: Segment Operating Income / Segment Revenue

### Geographic Segmentation
- Revenue and profitability by geographic region
- Important for multinational companies

## Non-GAAP Metrics

### Common Adjustments
- **Adjusted EBITDA**: EBITDA + Specific one-time items
- **Non-GAAP Net Income**: Excludes stock-based compensation, amortization, etc.
- **Constant Currency**: Removes foreign exchange impact

### Use with Caution
- Always reconcile to GAAP metrics
- Understand what is being excluded and why
- Compare non-GAAP definitions across companies

## Special Situations

### One-Time Items
- Restructuring charges
- Asset write-downs
- Legal settlements
- Acquisition-related costs

### Extraordinary Items
- Discontinued operations
- Significant accounting changes

## Analysis Guidelines

1. **Trend Analysis**: Look at 3-5 year patterns, not single data points
2. **Peer Comparison**: Compare metrics across industry competitors
3. **Guidance Context**: Compare actuals vs. company guidance
4. **Quality of Earnings**: Assess sustainability of reported profits
5. **Cash vs. Accrual**: Compare net income to operating cash flow
